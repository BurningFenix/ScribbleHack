My Knight

They flock to the knight on the horse
whose armor shines so bright,
white like the sun on the water,
an everglowing light

They smile as he rides through town,
another battle won.
He stands tall, noble and just,
as their eyes twinkle in the sun.

But my knight has just returned,
his armor falling from his shoulders.
Compared to the knight in the shining armor,
he's a failure as a soldier.

Blood leaks through his teeth,
his eye is red and swollen,
mud stains the once white armor,
its straps are worn and broken.

They judge him for his weakness,
they can't see past the scars.
But every night, he goes to sleep,
his eyes turned towards the stars.
